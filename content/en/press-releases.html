IMPORTANT INFORMATION concerning COPA AIRLINES FLIGHT CM 9979

PANAMA, October 14, 2016– Copa Airlines reports that preliminary information indicates that flight CM9979 was involved in an incident at 11:50 a.m. today at Tocumen International Airport.

The aircraft, a Boeing 737-800, registration HP3345 operating the route between Santiago, Chile and Panama City, Panama, departed at 6:19 a.m. (Chile Standard Time). The aircraft was carrying 144 passengers and 6 crew.

The airline is currently working with the authorities to compile all pertinent information. The appropriate authority will release all information related to the incident investigation, while Copa Airlines will provide information about the status of the passengers and crew, as well as assistance to family members.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION concerning COPA AIRLINES FLIGHT CM 9979

PANAMA, October 14, 2016 – Copa Airlines (deeply regrets confirmation of an accident involving flight CM9979 at approximately 11:55 a.m. today. The aircraft, model B737-800, with registration HP3345 was in route from Santiago, Chile, to Panama City, Panama. It departed Arturo Merino Benítez de Chile Airport at 6:19 a.m. The accident occurred in Panama City, Panama. The aircraft was carrying 144 passengers and 6 crew.

Preliminary information indicates that the accident occurred at 11:50 a.m. as the aircraft landed on runway 03 on the left of Tocumen International Airport.

Copa Airlines currently is directing all its efforts toward providing assistance to the surviving passengers and crew on board flight CM9979, and in contacting and providing assistance to their family members.

Copa Airlines’ Emergency Response Team is working closely with the authorities in charge of investigating the accident and is making available all necessary resources in order to establish its cause. The investigation and all communication concerning the findings will be handled by the proper authority.

PRELIMINARY INFORMATION ON COPA AIRLINES FLIGHT CM9979 INVOLVED IN ACCIDENT TODAY

PANAMA, October 14, 2016- Copa Airlines deeply regrets to announce that preliminary information indicates 5 of the 150 passengers on board Copa flight CM9979 involved in today’s accident have died. Another 35 passengers and crew members have been taken to medical centers for evaluation, while 110 passengers did not suffer any injuries.

Flight CM 9979 was being operated with a Boeing 737-800, registration HP3345 and was carrying a total of 144 passengers and 6 crew. The passengers represented 7 different nationalities: Argentina, Panama, Colombia, Costa Rica, Ecuador, Guatemala and the United States.

Everyone at Copa Airlines is deeply affected by this event. Our thoughts and prayers are with the passengers and crew of flight CM 9979 and their families. The airline has made all its resources available to the injured passengers and crew, and is contacting family members to provide any assistance they might need. In addition, a specially trained Copa Airlines team is actively working with the authorities in charge of the situation and the investigation to establish the cause of the accident.
